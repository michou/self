Where does testing start and design end?

I want to share with you a thought that cropped up in my mind after reading a designer's ranton current trends in web typography. If you wonder what does it have to do with testing, that's what the next 5 minutes will be about.

vs.

(5s) I think most of use have quite different images in their heads when thinking about a tester and about a designer - be it an experience designer, a UI designer or a graphic designer. What you see on this slide is pretty much how they look like in my head.

Everything is designed

The thing is, design is everywhere. Whenever you interact with something man-made, some kind of intent and some kind of design is present. Whether you're delighted by the design (as with a Ferrari or an iPhone) or never even think about it, like with the door handles in your home, it's there. But just as design is sometimes obvious (for good or for bad) and sometimes un-noticed, just the same the design (as an activity, this time) is sometimes deliberate and sometimes a mere by-product.

Unintentional design has no known authors

The problem with unintentional (or non-deliberate) design is that nobody is consciously thinking about it (duh). Most often this is the consequence of somebody that is not a designer, not thinking about designing the thing they create, but still designing it nonetheless. Such design is usually driven by gut feeling or experience.

What is a tester anyway?

But if you think about it, no matter your definition of a tester, I bet it has to do with users and your product. As such, I believe testers are best positioned to, at least, start talking about design.

Why should testers design?

And I don't necessarily mean you have to learn words like 'kerning' and 'fuchsia' and 'Bezier curve'. Even if you work on an API, a web-service, an internal CLI tool - think about how would your product work best for its users, for your clients or your customers. You already know the product, the users, their use-cases.

Don’t be the
“This is not my job”–guy

(it is everyone’s job)

Just as quality isn't just a tester's job, design can and should be everyone's job, or at least a real, living thing for everyone in your organization. It's just that you, as a tester, are best positioned to start a discussion about it, to help people turn unintentional design into a deliberate thinking process.
